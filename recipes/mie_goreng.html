Mie Goreng

Description

Mie Goreng is a chinese Indonesian dish that are said to be good luck, due to it signifying a long life!

It is a dry noodle dish that requires you to separately fry some ingredients, keeping the pan hot between swaps.

Note: This is recipe serves 4 people.

Ingredients

  • 5 packets of Indomie Mie Goreng (including all seasoning packets)
  • 180g chicken tenderloins or chicken thigh (cut into small pieces)
  • 1 teaspoon cornflour/fine cornstarch
  • Salt and white pepper
  • 4 tablespoons kecap manis (Indonesian sweet soy sauce)
  • 4 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 cloves of garlic (peeled and diced)
  • 100g onion (sliced)
  • 100g carrot (julienned)
  • 150g shredded cabbage
  • 3 slices fresh ginger
  • 150g shelled raw prawns
  • 2 sticks green onions/shallots (cut into inch long pieces)
  • Fried eggs (to top)
  • Chilli sauce, slices of cucumber and tomato (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bring water to a boil in a saucepan
  2. Add noodles and cook until tender (approx. 2 minutes)
  3. Mix chicken with cornflour, some salt and white pepper and set aside
  4. Mix kecap manis, oyster sacue, soy sauce and garlic with the seasoning packets from the Indomie packets (less seasoning power)
  5. Fry chicken in oil in a large wok on high heat. Remove from pan when done
  6. Add onion, carrot, cabbage and ginger, adding more oil and fry until softened
  7. Add prawns and fry until they start to turn pink
  8. Add cooked noodles and green onions and fry for a minute
  9. Sprinkle some seasoning powder from the Indomie packets
  10. Stir together and coat in the sauce
  11. Plate by topping with some eggs, chilli sauce, cucumber and tomato if desired
